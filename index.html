The problem

Solar panels lose up to 30% of their power
under partial shading.

01

Non-linear I-V characteristics

Solar cells only achieve 10–15% efficiency in isolation. Partial shading fractures the P–V curve into multiple local maxima, leaving conventional trackers locked on the wrong peak.

02

Distributed MPPT is expensive

Per-module converters recover the loss but triple the hardware count, cost, thermal budget and failure surface — commercially unviable for small-scale residential systems.

03

Complex algorithms don't converge

PSO, ANN and cuckoo-search methods require expensive processors, training data, and still fail to guarantee convergence under real-world time-varying irradiance.

The solution

1 → 3

The TDM MPPT controller time-shares a single synchronous buck converter across three PV modules, switching each into the power path sequentially. Every module gets its own tracked operating point — without 3× the hardware, cost, or heat.

How it works

Three modules. One loop.
Milliseconds apart.

01 — Sample

Each module switched in sequence

The ESP32 time-multiplexes each PV module into the converter in turn. The dual MCP3208 ADCs capture I/V pairs at 100k samples/sec, filtered through hardware RC stages to suppress 300 kHz PWM switching noise.

02 — Track

P&O identifies the peak

A per-module Perturb & Observe algorithm running under FreeRTOS uses the latest I/V samples to determine whether to increase or decrease duty cycle. Converges within 15 iterations under real PV conditions.

03 — Deliver

Power extracted, battery charged

The synchronous buck duty cycle is adjusted per module, harvested energy fed to the 4Ah lead-acid battery via CC/CV charging logic. OLED displays live I/V readings per channel.

Phase evolution

Phase 1 PCB photo
  • Synchronous buck converter validated at 87% average efficiency, peak 90%
  • Confirmed converter feasibility with real PV input and battery load
  • Identified ESP32 internal ADC noise floor insufficient for MPPT accuracy
  • Amplifier offset errors at low current motivated Phase 2 redesign
Phase 2 · Current

Split architecture · Full system

3× identical SBC boards + central control board · Full SMD

  • Dedicated control board + 3× identical SBC daughter boards via ribbon connectors
  • INA240 current sense amplifier: 50× gain with PWM rejection
  • Dual MCP3208 external ADC: 100k samples/sec, 12 channels via SPI
  • FreeRTOS concurrent task scheduling for TDM switching logic
  • Fully SMD design: compact, cost-effective, manufacturable
